vet app for rapid test

This case study focused on how vets diagnose cancer in dogs. The goal was to design an application which could visualize result from a rapid test. We worked with a company which had a new technique of measuring blood.
The new technique makes it possible to discover some types of cancer in an earlier stage.

research plan Interview prototype
Interview with product owner themantic analysis Design studio
sketches
wireframe

Solution

The vet takes a blood sample and drop blood on the rapid test. Then he/she wait 10 minutes and then they point the mobile camera towards the rapid test. Finally, the screen will show result that can be transferred to the journal.

Today vets take blood sample and send them to a lab and haft to wait for result.
With a rapid test and an app for viewing result the procedure would be faster and more efficient.

Good to know!

Acceptance is built on user background, experience and the perceived usefulness and usability of the product .
The interface needs to be simple and only have the function needed for the vets to reach their goal, it needs to present the Specificity (measure of reliability) of the test


We also found that the image of the company behind the product affect the willingness to try new technique.

my forest drone application

My forest drone was a project together with The Swedish forest agency. We developed an interfacedesign for a mobile application that helps forest workers to plan and overview the forest. One of our main goals was to motivate workers to use drones in their workday. It was important to develop an interface that forest workers could understand.

Plan Understand Prototype Validate
research plan field study prototype evaluation
Interview with product owner interview & observations sketches user test
- Personas wireframe
Affinity diagram mockup
interactive prototype

Solution

With the application the user can overview the forest with the drone, and tag(label) objects that he/she are interested of. The app has only the most critical information needed for flying, such as battery strength, height of drone and direction of the drone. The application has different map layers because the map layers view different information.

Good to know!

It is important to feel in control when flying the drone. Workers in the forest are not use to new technology and needs an application that is easy to use and understand. The project owner had a plan of gathering image material to train a AI of identifying objects when flying. However there is a fear of feeling watched from users. Users could feel less afraid if they had more control over what is happening. Use of transparency is one way of giving more control to users.

mobile Journalistic Tool

The case study is about understanding a journalist activities and how the mobile can support the journalist in their workprocess.

Solution

This mobile application supports journalists to write, tag and publish news on the go. It supports journalist to share material quick and easy with a common file library. This wireframe are based on the company´s existing writing tool and the case study.

Good to know!

The common attitude to work with the mobile as a tool are positiv. But not writing with it.
A mobile application could support an journalist on the way toward an assignment or when he or she happen to be at the spot of an event. The value of an mobile application is in the quick way of communicating and sharing information
